Stanley Kubrick

The greatest film director of the 20th century

Here's a timeline of Kubrick's life:

  • 1928 - Born July 26 in Manhattan, New York City, to a Jewish family.

  • 1946 - After finishing William Howard Taft High School became an apprentice photographer for Look and later a full-time staff photographer.

  • 1948 - Kubrick married his high-school girlfriend Toba Metz on May 28, when he was nineteen years old.

  • 1951 - Made first two short documentary films. Inspired by this early success, quit his job at Look. Divorced with Toba Metz.

  • 1955 - Married for the second time to a dancer and theatrical artist, Ruth Sobotka.

  • 1957 - With Paths of Glory, the film director Kubrick established himself not only as the leading commercial filmmaker of his generation but a world-class talent as well.

  • 1958 - After divorcing his second wife year before, Kubrick married Christiane Harlan. They had three children.

  • 1962 - To film Lolita, moved to England. And lived there until the end of his life.

  • 1968 - Kubrick released his most popular film, 2001: A Space Odyssey, with co-writing from Arthur C. Clarke. The film earned Kubrick 13 Academy Award nominations; he won one for his special effects work.

  • 1999 - On March 7, six days after screening a final cut of Eyes Wide Shut, Kubrick died in his sleep at the age of 70, after suffering a massive heart attack.
